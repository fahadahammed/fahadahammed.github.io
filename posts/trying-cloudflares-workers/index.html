Trying Cloudflares Workers

Created At: 2020-05-16 14:43

Category: technology Tags: technology, programming, cloudflare, wrangler, key-value

So, Cloud flare has a lot of options to provide. I personally and professionally been with them for a while. It has been a great journey. Recently, I was thinking about trying out their workers. I did today and it seems very very promising. The documentation was also good. wrangler is a nice idea and implementation. I wanted to try the concept and end up with static one.

If you hit here https://ip.tech0.xyz you will see your ip address. Pretty simple. It is running via workers.

$ curl -I https://ip.tech0.xyz

 HTTP/2 200 date: Sun, 17 May 2020 09:00:09 GMT content-type: text/plain;charset=UTF-8 content-length: 13 set-cookie: __cfduid=XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX; expires=Tue, 16-Jun-20 09:00:09 GMT; path=/; domain=.tech0.xyz; HttpOnly; SameSite=Lax expect-ct: max-age=604800, report-uri="https://report-uri.cloudflare.com/cdn-cgi/beacon/expect-ct" server: cloudflare cf-ray: XXXXXXXXXXX cf-request-id: XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX

But the problem is, I wanted to try key-value(KV) store of Cloudflares. But I am on zero luck as they do not provide it for free. It is costly from my points of view. $5/month.

However, I will give it a try when I will have some plans for my company. By the way, it is fast but it has limited calls. So if you try to flood that address, I will end up with error on serving.
